FAQ

Q) Why was FRESCO developed?
A) FRESCO replaces software developed for use in the ORA (Operational Range Assessment) program (ARAMS and TREECS) because of necessary computing, model, and date updates and upgrades.

Q) What is the Operational Range Assessment Program (ORAP)?
A) It is a DOD wide proactive effort to manage munition constituents to ensure long term range sustainability that is protective of human and ecological health.

Q) Is FRESCO only applicable to training ranges?
A) Although some of the tools within FRESCO are specifically geared toward screening training range sites for munition constituents, the reduced order modeling approaches within FRESCO can be applied to a variety of multimedia fate and transport questions.

Q) Do I need a special computer to run FRESCO?
A) No, FRESCO runs on ordinary PCs.

Q) Does FRESCO have any tools for modeling ordinary chemicals?
A.Yes, in addition to extensive chemical databases internal to FRESCO, users can add species and properties for emerging chemicals of interest.

Q) Can chemical benchmarks be customized?
A) Yes, users can create their own custom databases of both chemical properties and benchmark values within FRESCO.

Q) Do I need extensive site investigation to generate the input data to run a FRESCO model?
A) NO, FRESCO is designed as a screening tool that uses basic information about a site to build a model for estimating conservative chemical concentrations expected in response to range use.

Q) Does FRESCO cost anything to use?
A) No, FRESCO is FREE!
